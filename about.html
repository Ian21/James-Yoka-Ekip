Profile

2024

James Yoka Ekip

The man behind this website is James Yoka Ekip, a professionally trained accountant and self-made businessman from the Western Highlands of Papua New Guinea. Born into the Elti-Penambi group of Mount Hagen, James Yoka Ekip was educated in Western Highlands, East New Britain and Morobe provinces. He was raised in a Christian family and remains a devout Catholic. His philanthropy work started with the construction of a road connecting Ruki and Kenta to Kagamuga airport. He is a patron of Hagen art and craft, Western highlands history, Mount Hagen Golf Club, Andakelka coalition-style alliance, and various cultural and sporting events. Well educated and a good student of history and politics, James understands the obstacles to progress, and knows what it will take to put our country back on the right path of development in order to achieve our national goals enshrined in the Preamble of the Constitution. James Yoka Ekip has travelled extensively around the world in search of inspiration. Singapore, with its remarkable transformation from a backward economy to world leader in finance and industry, impresses JYE considerably. He knows that Singapore or Hong Kong can be replicated. Yet, he knows that discipline and resolute commitment to development objectives can transform communities, provinces, and countries. Great countries are built by great leaders – just as great countries are destroyed by tyrants. James Yoka Ekip is at his happiest when his people are happy. The joy that comes from the faces of ordinary people when a new road is opened is incomparable. Papua New Guineans in Mount Hagen, or Port Moresby, can walk around with their heads high, with smiles on their faces, when their basic needs are met by a responsible government with great men at the helm. James Yoka Ekip is connoisseur of good food, wine, music, and art. He likes Western cuisine, hot and spicy Asian dishes, as well as local PNG dishes. Coffee is one of his favourite beverages. He has installed expensive coffee making machines at his hotels and enjoys brewing and drinking coffee made from locally roasted Arabica coffee produced in the Highlands. He loves both classical music as well as country ballads. He collects Hagen artefacts – shields and gold-lipped pearl shells. He collects and raises birds and tree kangaroos at his Flyers Inn at Kagamuga. In addition to his refined taste for the finer things of life,

James Yoka Ekip has an extraordinary knowledge of world history. He can tell you about the rise and fall of the Roman Empire, the Sumerian and Egyptian civilizations, the French Revolution, the US Civil War, the two great world wars, and about the establishment of many countries in Europe, Africa and Asia. Hagen culture, based on 10,000 years of agriculture and shaped by prestige institutions, has been thoroughly studied by world renowned social anthropologists, Professor Andrew Strathern of Pittsburg University and Dame Professor Marilyn Strathern of Cambridge University, but James Yoka Ekip fears that our children are growing up in a world without cultural values. The development of agriculture at Kuk, near Mount Hagen, enabled the establishment of the great Hagen moka ceremonial exchange system, upon which the classic New Guinea big-man leadership system is based on. Rather than inheriting leadership from father to son under hereditary systems in the Trobriand Islands and in Polynesia, the Hagen big-man system is a meritocracy where anyone with entrepreneurial skills can acquire leadership status. Hagen is thus the home of pure democracy. Now in his fifties, James is acutely aware of the fact that time flies, as he plans to build a legacy of grand proportions. The accountant is under no illusion that building awe-inspiring monuments is a costly enterprise. James Yoka Ekip dreams of creating the opportunity for our children to learn about positive values, traditional culture, business skills, and about finding ways to improve their lives. James Yoka Ekip has contested the Hagen Open seat in the National Elections several times. He stood for elections in 2012, 2017, and 2022. The 2027 elections will be his last attempt to win a seat in the PNG National Parliament. Win or lose, James Yoka Ekip will continue to serve the people of Mount Hagen and Western highlands through his philanthropy. He has a house in Port Moresby, where he lives with his family, but his heart is in Mount Hagen, where his Elti-Penambi and Andakelka people live. His birthplace, Ruki-orong, a haven of peace and tranquility, is where he goes with his family during Christmas holidays to recharge his batteries after an hectic year!
